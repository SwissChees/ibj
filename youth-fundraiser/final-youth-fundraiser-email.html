International Bridges to Justice Youth Charter Defenders (YCDs) protecting and defending the rights of children in prison. Hear their stories through Spoken Word Poetry and a Panel!

YCD Noel Mbungu, 25 years old, DRC

"Every child deserves a chance, a clear morning, without pain, without bars, without iron chains. Let's give them justice, let's give them hope, let's hold out our hand in support."

Noel's Impact: Nearly 3,000 displaced children made aware of children's rights

YCD Anne-Bella, 24 years old, Burundi

"A child without rights is a tree without sap, A bird without sky, a dream without dreams. But a child who is protected becomes a forest, A source of shade, life, and humanity."

Anne-Bella's Impact: 17 children released from prisons

OUR MISSION
In recognition of the fundamental principles of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights, International Bridges to Justice (IBJ) is dedicated to protecting the basic legal rights of individuals in countries around the world. Specifically, IBJ works to guarantee all citizens the right to competent legal representation, the right to be protected from cruel and unusual punishment, and the right to a fair trial.
